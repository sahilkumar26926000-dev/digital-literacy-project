o Social Media Do’s for a college student:

* Network Professionally: Use Platforms like LinkedIn to connect with professor, alumni, and industry professionals in your field.
* Verify Information: Fact-check news stories or controversial claims before sharing them or prevent the spread of misinformation.
* Give credit where it’s Due: Always tag or mention original creators when sharing content, photos, or ideas that aren’t yours.
* Keep Privacy Setting Tight: Regularly check your security settings to control who can see your personal information and posts.
* Tink Before you post: Consider if a post could negatively affect your future career or reputation before hitting “send”.


o Social Media Don’t for a college student:

* Don’t badmouth others: Refrain from posting negative comments about your university, professors, classmates, or current/past employers.
* Don’t post illegal or Risky Acts: Avoid sharing photos or videos of underage drinking, drug use, or any activity that violates university policy.
* Don’t Plagiarize content: never pass off someone else’s work, design, or captions as your original content.
* Don’t overshare: avoid posting sensitive information like your home address, current precise location or private phone number.
* Don’t engage with trolls: avoid getting heated, public arguments or responding to accounts that are intentionally provocative.
